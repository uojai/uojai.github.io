Proposal Development Guidelines

This page provides a step-by-step guidelines to help students brainstorm, develop, and prepare a proposal for an AI project aimed at solving local problems in Juba, South Sudan. The timeline for this project is 2 months, and we encourage the use of state-of-the-art AI technologies to create impactful solutions. Let’s get started!

Key Dates

  • Group Submission Deadline: February 10, 2025
    • Submission Link: https://forms.gle/H6NbdxVqxMHSLgfW9
  • Proposal Review: February 11 - 20, 2025
  • AI Day Event: May 2025

Guidelines for Brainstorming and Proposal Development

1. Understand the Problem Space

  • Research Local Challenges: Identify pressing issues in Juba, South Sudan, such as healthcare access, education, agriculture, transportation, energy, or waste management.
  • Engage with the Community: Conduct interviews or surveys with local residents to understand their needs and challenges.
  • Narrow Down the Problem: Choose a specific, well-defined problem that can be addressed within the 2-month timeline.

2. Brainstorm AI Solutions

  • Explore State-of-the-Art AI Technologies: Research cutting-edge AI tools and techniques, such as:
    • Computer Vision (e.g., object detection, image classification)
    • Natural Language Processing (NLP) (e.g., chatbots, sentiment analysis)
    • Predictive Analytics (e.g., forecasting, decision-making models)
    • Generative AI (e.g., text, image, or video generation)
    • Reinforcement Learning (e.g., optimization for resource allocation)
  • Think Creatively: Combine AI technologies with local context to propose innovative solutions.
  • Feasibility Check: Ensure the solution is realistic and achievable within the timeline and available resources.

3. Develop Your Proposal

Your proposal should include the following sections:

  1. Executive Summary: Briefly describe the problem, proposed solution, and expected impact.
  2. Problem Statement: Clearly define the local problem you are addressing.
  3. Proposed Solution: Explain how your AI solution works and specify the AI technologies and tools you plan to use.
  4. Implementation Plan: Break down the 2-month timeline into phases.
  5. Expected Impact: Describe how your solution will benefit the local community.
  6. Challenges and Mitigation Strategies: Identify potential challenges and propose strategies to overcome them.
  7. Team Roles and Responsibilities: Assign roles to team members and highlight their contributions.

4. Use State-of-the-Art Tools and Frameworks

  • AI Frameworks: TensorFlow, PyTorch, Hugging Face, OpenAI API.
  • Data Collection Tools: Google Forms, KoboToolbox, or local surveys.
  • Cloud Platforms: Google Cloud, AWS, or Azure for model training and deployment.
  • Collaboration Tools: GitHub, Slack, or Trello for team coordination.

5. Prepare for AI Day 2025

  • Prototype Development: Build a working prototype or proof of concept to demonstrate your solution.
  • Presentation: Prepare a 10-minute presentation showcasing your problem, solution, and results.
  • Poster or Demo: Create a visually engaging poster or live demo for the event.

Tips for Success

  • Start Early: Use the 2-month timeline wisely to avoid last-minute rushes.
  • Collaborate: Work closely with your team and leverage each member’s strengths.
  • Seek Mentorship: Reach out to AI experts, or local organizations for guidance.
  • Focus on Impact: Prioritize solutions that have a tangible, positive impact on the community.
